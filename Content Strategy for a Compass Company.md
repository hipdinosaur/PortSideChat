# Content Strategy for a Compass Company
### Built from Backcountry Marketing Podcast Principles --- 

## The Foundation: Know Your Audience First Before creating a single piece of content, the podcast transcripts are clear about where to start. As Marc Bock from Gore-Tex states: *"If you have a good understanding of your target audience, you can be specific and you can be precise... you don't have to be super loud with that. You just have to be aware, okay, this is the right content for the right target audience on the right channel."* **Research questions to answer first:** - Who actually buys compasses today? (Hikers, hunters, military, scouts, navigators, survivalists?) - Who are the buyers of tomorrow? (Younger generation who may default to GPS) - What do they value beyond the product itself? --- ## The Three-Bubble Framework Marc Bock's strategic framework is directly applicable here: *"How can we bring together our corporate needs with the target audience needs and the channel needs... only if you have a big overlap of these three bubbles can you create relevant content."* **For a compass company:** | Corporate Needs | Audience Needs | Channel Needs | |----------------|----------------|---------------| | Differentiate from GPS/digital navigation | Connection to nature, self-reliance, skill building | Long form for YouTube (10+ min performs well) | | Build brand identity | Adventure inspiration | Vertical content for Instagram/TikTok | | Attract younger buyers | Authenticity, real stories | Email/blog for deeper engagement | | Justify premium pricing | Community belonging | Live/physical events | --- ## Content Pillars ### 1. SKILL & CRAFT (Educate) The compass is one of humanity's oldest navigation tools. Own that expertise. - "Dead Reckoning" video series teaching traditional navigation - "No Signal, No Problem" content showing real scenarios where digital fails - Partnership with wilderness schools, scouts, military veterans **Why this works:** Chris Burkard reminds us that brands need a clear identity. *"You don't need to be everything to everybody. There's never been a single editor at a magazine that's hired somebody worth the money because they were good at decent at portraiture and animal photography and weddings and action sports. They hire you because you're a specialist."* Your compass company should own navigation mastery. --- ### 2. STORY-FIRST CONTENT (Inspire) Following the Gore-Tex Breaking Trails model — find humans who have overcome barriers using navigation skills. Story angles to pursue: - A blind hiker who navigates by compass and terrain feel - Search and rescue volunteers who rely on compasses when technology fails - Indigenous navigators who blend traditional wayfinding with modern tools - A solo sailor crossing an ocean with compass as primary tool *"We are really looking for stories where people have overcome certain challenges or barriers... sometimes it's more interesting to tell a story about overcoming barriers and challenges instead of just telling how someone has won the biggest race."* — Marc Bock --- ### 3. BRAND VALUES (Connect) As Chris Burkard notes, in today's market the product is just the entry fee: *"The entry fee just to get into the industry is that you have to make a jacket that keeps you waterproof... they're looking for a brand they identify with... You latch onto these brands because you feel like they're speaking to you."* A compass company's values content could center on: - Anti-digital dependency / digital detox movement - Environmental stewardship (navigate without leaving a trace) - Self-reliance and mental resilience - Preservation of traditional skills --- ### 4.
